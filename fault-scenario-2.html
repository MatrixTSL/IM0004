Fault Scenario 2 - Cleanroom Temperature Control System

Scenario Overview

Company: Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Inc.
Process: Cleanroom HVAC temperature control
Shift: Production shift during peak hours
Urgency: High - Product quality at risk
Difficulty: Intermediate
Time: 30-45 minutes

Background

You're working in a pharmaceutical cleanroom where temperature control is critical for product quality. The HVAC system has been struggling to maintain the required 22°C ±1°C temperature range.

Recently, production has increased by 40% due to a new contract, and the cleanroom temperature has been fluctuating between 24-26°C. The quality control team has reported that some batches are showing temperature-related quality issues.

The HVAC system shows normal operation, but the temperature readings indicate the system is running at maximum capacity and still can't meet the setpoint.

System Status

Setpoint: 22°C
Current Temperature: 25°C
Tolerance: ±1°C
HVAC Load: 95%
Production Increase: +40%
Quality Issues: YES
Alarm Status: HIGH TEMP

Learning Objectives

  • Understand HVAC system capacity limitations
  • Analyze the impact of increased production load
  • Identify system design vs. operational issues
  • Develop solutions for capacity constraints

Investigation Tools

Check Heat Load Analysis

Analyze current heat generation from equipment and personnel

Verify Chiller Capacity

Check chiller system specifications and current performance

Analyze Production Schedule

Review production timeline and equipment usage patterns

Inspect Airflow Distribution

Check air distribution system and ductwork efficiency

Investigation Results

Analysis Questions

Question 1: What is the primary cause of the temperature control failure?

Question 2: What immediate actions can be taken to stabilize the temperature?

Question 3: What long-term solutions should be implemented?
