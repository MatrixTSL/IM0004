Fault Scenario 2 - Pharmaceutical Flow System

Fault Scenario 2: Pharmaceutical Flow System

Company: Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Plant
Process: Sterile liquid solution production for IV bags
Shift: Production shift start
Urgency: High - Production blocked by low-flow alarm
Difficulty: Intermediate
Time: 20-30 minutes

Background

You work in a pharmaceutical plant that produces sterile liquid solutions for IV bags. The flow system is critical for maintaining precise fluid levels in mixing tanks. As your shift starts, the production team reports a low-flow alarm preventing the system from running.

The supervisor mentions that the flow was fine earlier, but now the pump seems to be running at full speed with barely any flow. The system is designed to maintain precise fluid levels for pharmaceutical production, and any interruption could affect product quality and sterility.

The flow sensor is showing minimal flow despite the pump running at maximum capacity. This suggests either a blockage, pump failure, or sensor malfunction that needs immediate attention.

Industry Context: In pharmaceutical manufacturing, flow systems are critical for maintaining precise fluid levels and ensuring product quality. Low-flow conditions can indicate pump wear, blockages, or sensor issues that could affect production schedules and product sterility.

System Status

Flow Rate: LOW
Pump Speed: 100%
Flow Sensor: MINIMAL FLOW
System Operation: BLOCKED
Production Status: STOPPED
Alarm Status: LOW FLOW ALARM

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Check for blockages in the flow system and piping
  • Verify pump performance and mechanical condition
  • Test flow sensor accuracy and calibration
  • Inspect for air entrainment or cavitation issues
